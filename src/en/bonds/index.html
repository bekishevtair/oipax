Bonds

Bonds serve as financial instruments issued by governments and corporations to raise funds for various purposes, including financing new projects or covering operational costs. When entities require capital, issuing bonds becomes one of the available options to secure the necessary funds. Investors who purchase bonds essentially lend money to the issuing entity and receive regular interest payments along with the return of the principal amount at maturity.

Bonds are widely recognized for their reliability as fixed-income assets, as they offer a fixed interest rate to debtholders. This characteristic contributes to the bond market's relatively lower volatility compared to other financial markets. It's important to note that all bonds have a predetermined maturity date, marking the end of the loan term and the obligation to fully repay the principal amount. This predetermined maturity date provides clarity for both issuers and investors regarding the investment's duration and the timing of principal repayment.
