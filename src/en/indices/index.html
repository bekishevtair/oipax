Indices

Indices, also referred to as indexes, represent distinct segments of the financial market. Beginners often find themselves perplexed by terms like S&P 500, Nasdaq 100, and Hang Seng. Let's take the Nasdaq 100 index as an example. It serves as a stock market index encompassing the 100 largest companies listed on the Nasdaq stock market, including prominent names like PayPal, Starbucks, Amazon, Baidu, and Apple.

By opting to trade indices, you gain access to a diverse range of market segments on the global stage. This approach allows you to benefit from the fluctuations in the entire market, rather than being solely exposed to the volatility of a single stock. It offers an opportunity to capitalize on both upward and downward movements in the market.

What are the advantages of Indices?

  • Global Markets
  • Portfolio Diversification
  • Low margins, bigger exposure
  • Tight spreads
