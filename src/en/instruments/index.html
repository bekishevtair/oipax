What is a CFD?

A CFD (contract for difference) is a contract between two parties, a ‘buyer‘ and a ‘seller‘, which orders the seller to pay the buyer the difference between the current value of an asset and its value at the time of the contract. If the difference is a negative number, the buyer pays the seller instead. CFDs are financial derivatives that allow investors to benefit from an increase (long position) or to benefit from a decrease (short position) in the prices of an underlying financial instrument.

With CFDs, you can also use leverage : the broker lends a client capital, allowing the client to trade with more capital than he has available in his trading account (this means that profits, but also potential losses, increase).

Commodities

A commodity is any raw material (e.g. silver, gold) or primary agricultural product (e.g. wheat, coffee, cocoa) that you can buy or sell around the world. If demand increases, the price of the commodity increases. Conversely, if demand decreases, the price of the commodity decreases.

Commodity price movements directly reflect global events and therefore the commodity market is highly volatile. With proper monitoring of the markets and commodities, investing in commodities can yield favourable results. However, it is important to examine the risks as well. Under certain market conditions and at certain times, commodity prices can rise significantly, making them a wise investment option for a diverse portfolio.
